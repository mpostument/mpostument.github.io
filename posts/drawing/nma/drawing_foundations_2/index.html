Cultivating Creativity: Unveiling My Odyssey Through Drawing Foundations 2

Embark on an artistic odyssey as I recount my experience with the challenging Drawing Foundations 2 course. Join me in exploring the highs and lows of my creative journey, from mastering gesture drawings to delving into the intricate play of light and shadow. Witness the evolution of my artistry and the relentless pursuit of improvement with each stroke of the pencil.

Hey Friends!

I’m thrilled to announce that I’ve successfully conquered another inspiring chapter of my artistic journey – the captivating Drawing Foundations 2 course. This course, by far, has proven to be the most formidable challenge I’ve undertaken. Yet, with every challenge comes the promise of growth. As I reflect on this course, I can’t help but anticipate the rewarding struggles that await me in each subsequent endeavor.

Nestled within this course are the fruits of my dedication – a collection of drawings that I’ve painstakingly crafted. While some of these creations may still be a work in progress, particularly my gesture drawings, I’ve embarked on a ritual of daily practice. The goal is simple: to produce one or more gesture drawings daily, a practice that I’m convinced will inch me closer to mastery. It’s true, they’re not perfect yet, but therein lies the beauty of this artistic journey – the pursuit of progress.

As I unveil my drawings to you, dear friends, I invite you to witness my evolution. My artistry may be a work in progress, but it’s the persistence and determination that truly count. Stay with me as I delve deeper into the nuances of artistic expression and embrace the challenge of improving with every stroke of my pencil.

Lesson 1 Introduction to Gesture Drawing with Bill Perkins, Catherine Bobkoski, John Asaro#

The course kicked off with gesture drawing led by the skilled trio – Bill Perkins, Catherine Bobkoski, and John Asaro. This foundational exercise was a rollercoaster. While some of my gesture drawings showed promise, others left much to be desired. But isn’t that the beauty of learning? I committed to daily practice, embracing the struggle as an essential step towards progress.

Lessons 2 Drawing Plaster Casts with Mark Westermoe#

Surprisingly, the next lesson on drawing plaster casts with Mark Westermoe felt less challenging than gesture drawing. I noticed an improvement in my approach, though room for growth remained. My drawings were a testament to my evolving understanding of form and structure.

Lessons 3 Refining Your Plaster Cast Drawing with Mark Westermoe#

Adding shadows to my plaster cast drawings in Lesson 3 was a game-changer. Mark Westermoe’s guidance illuminated the power of shading in adding depth and realism to my artwork. These drawings marked a pivotal point in my journey, showcasing a newfound grasp of lighting and contrast.

Lessons 4 Understanding and Using Value with Bill Perkins#

Bill Perkins’ lessons on value were an eye-opener. Analyzing images, creating value charts, and applying them to landscapes presented a fresh challenge. My attempts at value-based landscape drawings were a mix of triumphs and setbacks, but the process deepened my appreciation for the interplay of light and shadow.

Lesson 5 Introduction to Major and Minor Keys with Bill Perkins#

Major and minor keys? I admit, this lesson had me scratching my head. Analyzing other drawings and creating a major/minor key matrix was a demanding task. The struggle was real, but the journey was undeniably enlightening, pushing me to grasp complex artistic concepts.

Lesson 6 Using Value in Landscape Drawing with Bill Perkins, Catherine Bobkoski#

In the final leg of the course, I delved into creating Notan and Chiaroscuro matrices, culminating in landscape drawings that incorporated these principles. The outcome was a series of drawings that not only highlighted my progress but also echoed the artistic theories that had once seemed elusive.

A Glimpse into the Future#

With Drawing Foundations 2 behind me, I’m brimming with excitement for what lies ahead. As I step into more advanced courses, I carry with me the lessons learned, the progress made, and the hunger for continuous improvement. Remember, fellow artists, every stroke of the pencil is a step towards mastering our craft.

Here’s to the artistry that lies within us all, waiting to be uncovered stroke by stroke, lesson by lesson.
